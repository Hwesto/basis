### What Closes The Gap?

Only one policy transformation changes the arithmetic fundamentally:

**EU single market access.** The NBER (November 2025) estimates 
Brexit has already reduced GDP by 6-8%. Recovering even half of 
this through single market membership would generate £50-100bn 
in additional economic activity, yielding £15-30bn in additional 
tax revenue at current rates. Combined with domestic tax reform 
(£17-40bn), this brings total additional revenue to £32-70bn — 
still insufficient for the 5% defence scenario but transformative 
for the 3.5% scenario.

No party states this. Labour's red lines (no single market, no 
customs union, no freedom of movement) accept permanent economic 
damage that constrains every other domain's funding. The graph 
makes this dependency chain visible:

> EU trade access → GDP → tax revenue → funds NHS, housing, 
> education, defence, welfare, justice, environment

This is the single most important finding in the manifesto.

## META-FINDING 2: EVERY SYSTEM FAILURE CASCADES

The graph reveals dependency chains that no single-domain analysis 
can capture. The major cascades:

### The Housing-Welfare-NHS Cascade
```
Housing undersupply → high housing costs → housing benefit rises
                   → cold homes → respiratory illness → NHS costs
                   → fuel poverty → welfare spending
                   → overcrowding → educational underperformance
                   → homelessness → temporary accommodation (£1.7bn/yr)
```

### The Education-Immigration-NHS Cascade
```
Education underinvestment → skills shortages → immigration dependency
                        → NHS workforce relies on 20% foreign staff
                        → Social care workforce relies on 19% foreign staff
                        → Immigration restrictions → workforce crisis
                        → NHS waits → long-term sickness → welfare costs
```

### The Court-Prison-Welfare Cascade
```
Court defunding → backlog grows → remand population grows (20% of prisoners)
              → prison overcrowding → early release → reoffending (48%)
              → more court cases → more prison → more welfare costs
```

### The Taxation-Everything Cascade
```
Tax locks (no IT/NI/VAT rises) → fiscal constraint
    → NHS underfunded → waits grow → sickness benefit rises
    → Schools underfunded → skills gap → immigration dependency
    → Courts underfunded → backlog → prison crisis
    → Housing underfunded → undersupply → benefit costs
    → Environment underfunded → pollution → health costs
```

### The Brexit-Fiscal Cascade
```
Brexit → 4-8% GDP reduction → tax revenue loss (£30-60bn)
      → fiscal constraint on every domain
      → inadequate NHS/housing/education/defence funding
      → system failures cascade through all domains above
```

These cascades prove that domain-siloed policymaking fails. The 
graph makes interdependencies computable.

## META-FINDING 3: THE CONSPIRACY OF SILENCE IS STRUCTURAL

The IFS phrase "conspiracy of silence" applies across every domain:

| Domain | What No Party States |
|---|---|
| Taxation | Tax locks prevent funding the services they promise |
| NHS | Every manifesto delivers the tightest NHS funding in history |
| Housing | 300,000/yr was never achieved without public sector building |
| Immigration | OBR: skilled migration = net fiscal positive. Caps hurt GDP |
| Defence | NATO 5% is "incompatible with a welfare state" (Spain PM) |
| Education | Post-16 funding cut 14-28%, not restored, not committed to |
| Welfare | Benefits rise because other systems fail, not generosity |
| EU/Trade | Single market membership = highest-return policy available |
| Environment | OEP: "largely off track" on all Environment Act targets |
| Justice | Neither party stated how they'd fund court/prison recovery |
| Electoral Reform | 57.8% unrepresented; FPTP incentivises dishonesty |

**The structural cause:** FPTP creates electoral incentives that reward 
dishonesty and punish truth-telling. Safe seats have no accountability. 
Swing seats reward populism. Tax locks, benefit caps, and immigration 
targets are calibrated for marginal constituencies, not evidence.

**The structural fix:** Proportional representation. Under PR, 
coalition governments must negotiate honest compromise. The 2024 PR 
projection (Lab 228, Con 139, Reform 100, Green 71) would require 
Labour to negotiate with Lib Dems or Greens — producing policy 
demonstrably closer to the evidence base identified across all 
12 domains.

Electoral reform is the upstream meta-domain. Change it and every 
downstream domain's political feasibility shifts.

## META-FINDING 4: EDUCATION IS THE ROOT NODE

The GNN would identify Education as the node with the most outgoing 
AFFECTS edges. Every workforce crisis traces back:

- NHS staffing → medical school places → Education
- Construction capacity → building skills → Education  
- Energy transition → retrofit training → Education
- Social care → care worker training → Education
- Immigration dependency → domestic skills gap → Education
- Welfare (in-work poverty) → low wages → skills shortage → Education

Heckman's finding anchors the CBA: early years investment returns 
7-10% annually — the highest ROI in any domain. Each additional year 
of education → 8-13% higher lifetime earnings. The evidence is 
unambiguous: education is the intervention point with the longest 
dependency chain and the highest compounding returns.

Yet: school funding fell 9% in real terms during austerity. College 
funding fell 14%. Neither major party committed to real-terms 
increases above inflation. The system that produces every other 
domain's workforce is running on fumes.

## META-FINDING 5: NATURE IS INFRASTRUCTURE, PRICED AT ZERO

The Dasgupta Review (HM Treasury, 2021) concluded the economy is 
embedded in nature, not external to it. But natural capital is 
valued at zero in every fiscal calculation:

- Air pollution: 26,000-38,000 equivalent deaths/year (CMO)
- Pollination: ~£700m/yr to UK agriculture
- Peatlands: 3.2 billion tonnes stored carbon
- Nature-based flood defence: 50-75% cheaper than engineered
- Biodiversity decline: systemic financial risk (Green Finance Institute)
- OEP: government "largely off track" on all Environment Act targets

The Greenpeace scoring reveals the political gap: Green 39/40, Lib 
Dem 32, Labour 21, Conservative 5. Reform would actively reverse 
protections. Environment loses every fiscal competition because 
nature has no lobby, no constituency in swing seats, and no 
short-term electoral payoff.
